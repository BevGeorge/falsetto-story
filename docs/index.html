Fellow music nerd Estelle Caswell at Vox told me about her theory: male voices, these days, seem really high.

Like this Shawn Mendes Jam.

Sure, today’s music isn’t full on Bee Gees.

But is 2019 a renaissance for high male voices in pop music?

When Estelle mentioned her theory, I pulled together vocal register data for any song that’s charted on the Billboard Hot 100.

The register data is pretty straightforward. Each song is rated on a 1-10 scale.

Currently playing is Ginuwine’s Pony, which peaked at #6 in 1996. It’s a 10 out of 10 on vocal register.

So if Pony is a 10, most Barry White jams are a 2 on the register scale.

Let’s build this chart with the 248 songs that first charted in 2019.

We’re only curious about male singing, so let’s focus on men and filter out any songs primarily spoken/rapped, which brings us to 80 songs.

The average register of these 80s songs from 2019 is 5.95, on a scale of 1 to 10. How does this compare historically?

This chart show’s average register since 1958. This year doesn’t look that impressive compared to the ‘80s
